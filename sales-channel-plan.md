# Sales Channel Strategy for HMRC Reporter Digital Products

## Recommended: Gumroad First, Then Scale

### Phase 1: Launch on Gumroad (Quick Start)

**Why Gumroad:**
- 10% fee - reasonable for digital products
- Built-in audience searching for templates/tools
- Instant setup - can be live in hours
- Handles all payments, delivery, receipts
- Mobile-friendly checkout

**Setup:**
1. Create Gumroad account
2. Create product collection "Tax Tools UK"
3. Upload products with pricing
4. Add preview descriptions from READMEs
5. Share link on social media

### Phase 2: Build Shopify Store (Month 2-3)

**Why Shopify:**
- More professional
- Better branding control
- Lower fees on higher volumes
- Email capture built-in

### Phase 3: Add Your Blog (Ongoing)

**Your blog already exists:** https://blog.hmrcreporter.com

**Strategy:**
- Add "Shop" section to blog
- Link products in relevant blog posts
- Product-specific landing pages

## Marketing Channels

### 1. Google Ads (Pay-Per-Click)
**Budget: £500-1000/month**
- Target keywords: "tax calculator UK", "landlord tax spreadsheet", "self assessment tools"
- Start with £20/day, adjust based on results

### 2. Content Marketing (SEO)
- Write blog posts targeting tax keywords
- Include product links in posts
- Example: "Best tax spreadsheets for landlords" → link to products

### 3. Email List
- Capture emails on blog (newsletter)
- Email list of past customers
- Promote new products

### 4. Social Media
- LinkedIn: Post in UK small business groups
- Twitter/X: Tax advice threads with product mentions
- Facebook: Landlord and property groups

### 5. Marketplace Presence
- Etsy: List products as "digital downloads"
- Teachers Pay Teachers: For educational tax guides
