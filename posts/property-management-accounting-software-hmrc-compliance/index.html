Property Management Accounting Software for HMRC Compliance

Last updated: March 2026

Property managers in the UK face a growing compliance challenge. Making Tax Digital is expanding, platform reporting rules have tightened, and HMRC now receives your income data directly from Airbnb and Booking.com. If your accounting software isn’t built for this, you’re at risk. Here’s how to choose property management accounting software that keeps you compliant with HMRC.

The Compliance Landscape Has Changed

Two major changes hit property managers in recent years:

1. Digital Platform Reporting (January 2024)

Platforms like Airbnb, Booking.com, and VRBO now report your earnings to HMRC automatically. They submit data using the OECD Digital Platform Reporting standard, including:

  • Gross income per seller
  • Number of transactions
  • Property details

HMRC matches this against your Self Assessment. If there’s a gap, you’ll get a letter.

2. Making Tax Digital (Rolling Out 2026–2028)

Making Tax Digital for Income Tax requires:

  • Digital record keeping
  • Quarterly updates to HMRC
  • MTD-compatible software

Timeline:

If you’re still using spreadsheets, you need to prepare now.

What Property Managers Need from Accounting Software

Property management isn’t like running a normal business. You need software that understands:

Property-Level Tracking

Every property has its own income, expenses, and tax position. Your software must track:

  • Income per property
  • Expenses per property
  • Ownership splits (if joint)
  • Personal use days

Multi-Platform Income

Most property managers receive income from multiple sources:

  • Airbnb
  • Booking.com
  • VRBO
  • Direct bookings
  • Long-term rentals

Your software should consolidate this into one view.

HMRC-Compliant Output

The end product matters most. Your software should produce:

  • Self Assessment figures (SA105 property pages)
  • Digital Platform Reporting XML (for OECD compliance)
  • MTD-compatible quarterly submissions

Expense Management

Property managers deal with many expense types:

  • Cleaning and maintenance
  • Insurance
  • Mortgage interest
  • Utilities
  • Agent fees
  • Repairs

Your software should categorise these correctly and track them per property.

The Right Tool Depends on Your Properties

Short-term rentals (Airbnb, Booking.com, VRBO)

You need HMRC Reporter. General accounting software can’t handle platform data formats, and Landlord Vision isn’t built for short-term lets.

Long-term rentals (traditional tenancies)

Landlord Vision or Xero will work. You don’t need platform import, and standard accounting features are sufficient.

Mixed portfolio (short-term and long-term)

Use HMRC Reporter for your short-term properties and a general accounting tool (Xero or QuickBooks) for long-term rentals and overall business accounting.

Preparing for Making Tax Digital

Regardless of which software you choose, take these steps now:

  1. Go digital — Move all records to software, not spreadsheets
  2. Track per property — HMRC wants property-level detail
  3. Keep receipts digitally — Photos or scans, not paper
  4. Choose MTD-compatible software — All tools listed above support MTD
  5. Start quarterly reporting — Practice now so you’re ready when it’s mandatory

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I use one software for all my property accounting? A: If you only have short-term rentals, HMRC Reporter covers your compliance needs. For broader accounting (payroll, invoicing), pair it with Xero or QuickBooks.

Q: Is Making Tax Digital mandatory for property managers? A: It will be. Starting April 2026 for property income over £50,000, expanding to all property income by 2028.

Q: What is Digital Platform Reporting? A: A global standard (OECD) requiring platforms like Airbnb to report seller earnings to tax authorities. In the UK, HMRC receives this data since January 2024.

Q: Do I need an accountant if I use accounting software? A: Software handles data and reporting. An accountant can advise on tax strategy, allowances, and complex situations. Many property managers use both.

Q: How do I handle properties I manage for other landlords? A: Use software that supports multi-owner tracking. HMRC Reporter lets you assign properties to different owners and generate separate reports.
