Tools to Track Airbnb Income for Tax Reporting in the UK

Last updated: March 2026

Tracking Airbnb income sounds easy — until you have multiple properties, several platforms, and HMRC expecting accurate reports. Most hosts start with a spreadsheet and quickly hit a wall. This guide covers the best tools to track Airbnb income for tax reporting, and what to look for when choosing one.

Why Tracking Airbnb Income Is Harder Than It Looks

Your Airbnb payout isn’t your taxable income. Here’s why:

  • Gross vs net — Airbnb deducts service fees before paying you. HMRC wants gross income with fees listed as a separate expense.
  • Multiple platforms — If you also list on Booking.com or VRBO, you need to combine income across all of them.
  • Expenses — Cleaning, maintenance, insurance, and mortgage interest all need tracking too.
  • Personal use — Days you use the property yourself affect your tax position.

The Spreadsheet Trap

A spreadsheet works when you have one property on one platform. But add a second property, a second platform, or currency conversions, and it becomes:

  • Time-consuming to maintain
  • Prone to errors
  • Hard to audit
  • Useless for HMRC XML output

And that’s before Making Tax Digital requirements kick in.

What a Good Tracking Tool Should Do

Core Features

  • Import from platforms — pull data from Airbnb, Booking.com, VRBO, etc.
  • Track gross income — before platform fees, not just payouts
  • Categorise expenses — link costs to specific properties
  • Handle multiple properties — separate records per property
  • Generate reports — Self Assessment figures or HMRC XML

Features That Save Time

  • Automatic property matching across platforms
  • Currency conversion for international bookings
  • Expense suggestions based on property type
  • Duplicate detection

How to Track Airbnb Income: A Simple System

If you’re not ready for software yet, here’s the minimum viable tracking system:

Step 1: Download Platform Reports Monthly

Export CSVs from every platform you list on. Save them in a folder by month.

Step 2: Record Gross Income

For each booking, record:

  • Gross amount (before fees)
  • Platform fees
  • Net payout

Step 3: Track Expenses

Keep receipts and record:

  • Cleaning costs
  • Maintenance and repairs
  • Insurance
  • Mortgage interest
  • Utilities (if you pay them)

Step 4: Note Personal Use Days

If you use the property yourself, record those dates. They affect your tax position.

Step 5: Calculate and Report

At year-end, total your income and expenses, and submit on your Self Assessment (SA105 property pages).

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I need to track income from each platform separately? A: Yes. HMRC needs total income across all platforms, but you should track each one separately for accuracy and audit purposes.

Q: What’s the difference between tracking gross and net income? A: Gross income is the total booking amount. Net income is what you receive after platform fees. HMRC wants gross, with fees claimed as an expense.

Q: Can I just use my bank statements? A: Your bank statements show net payouts, not gross income. You’ll miss the platform fees and won’t have the breakdown HMRC requires.

Q: How far back should I keep records? A: HMRC requires you to keep records for at least 5 years after the 31 January submission deadline.

Q: Does Making Tax Digital change what I need to track? A: Yes. MTD requires digital records and quarterly submissions. Spreadsheets may not be sufficient — you’ll need MTD-compatible software.
