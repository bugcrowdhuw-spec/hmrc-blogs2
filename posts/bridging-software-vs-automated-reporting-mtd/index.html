Bridging Software vs Automated Reporting: Which Is Right for MTD?

Last updated: March 2026

Making Tax Digital for Income Tax lands in April 2026. If you earn property income — from Airbnb, Booking.com, or direct lets — you’ll need digital records and quarterly submissions to HMRC. The question isn’t whether you need software. It’s which kind.

There are two approaches: bridging software that lets you keep your spreadsheets, and automated reporting that replaces them entirely. Both work. But they work very differently.

What Is Bridging Software?

Bridging software connects your existing spreadsheets to HMRC’s MTD system. You keep doing what you’ve always done — maintain your records in Excel or Google Sheets — and the bridging tool submits the data digitally.

Popular options include:

  • Absolute Accounting Income Tax Filler — around £48/year, links your spreadsheets to MTD submissions
  • Able — free tier available, designed for landlords
  • TaxCalc — established accountancy software with bridging capability

How It Works

  1. You maintain your income and expense records in a spreadsheet
  2. You map spreadsheet cells to MTD categories
  3. The bridging software reads your spreadsheet and submits to HMRC
  4. You repeat quarterly

The Pros

  • No workflow change — keep using your spreadsheets
  • Low cost — typically £50–£150 per year
  • Familiar — if your spreadsheets work, nothing else changes
  • No bank feed integration needed — you control what goes in

The Cons

  • Still manual — someone has to enter every transaction
  • Error-prone — copy-paste mistakes, missed entries, wrong categorisation
  • Time-consuming — the spreadsheet maintenance doesn’t go away
  • No cross-platform view — if you use Airbnb and Booking.com, you’re merging data manually
  • Gross vs net confusion — easy to record payouts instead of gross income

What Is Automated Reporting?

Automated reporting pulls your data directly from booking platforms and generates HMRC-ready reports. No spreadsheets, no manual entry, no mapping cells to categories.

How It Works

  1. You connect your Airbnb, Booking.com, or other platform accounts
  2. The software imports your booking data automatically
  3. It categorises income and expenses against HMRC requirements
  4. It generates quarterly MTD submissions and annual Self Assessment figures
  5. You review and submit

The Pros

  • No manual data entry — transactions pull automatically from platforms
  • Accurate gross figures — captures booking totals, not just payouts
  • Multi-platform — combines Airbnb, Booking.com, Vrbo, and direct bookings in one place
  • Time savings — hours per month vs spreadsheets
  • Audit trail — HMRC-ready records with source data linked

The Cons

  • Higher cost — typically more than bridging software
  • Requires platform access — you need to connect your accounts
  • Less customisation — structured approach, not freeform like a spreadsheet

When Bridging Software Makes Sense

Bridging software is a good fit if:

  • You have one or two properties
  • You use a single platform (e.g., Airbnb only)
  • Your spreadsheet is well-maintained and up to date
  • You’re comfortable with manual data entry
  • Budget is the primary concern
  • You have an accountant who manages your spreadsheets

It’s a minimum viable compliance approach. You meet MTD requirements without changing your process. For hosts with simple setups, that’s fine.

When Automated Reporting Makes Sense

Automated reporting is the better choice if:

  • You manage multiple properties
  • You list on multiple platforms (Airbnb, Booking.com, Vrbo)
  • You’re spending significant time on spreadsheet maintenance
  • You want to avoid the gross vs net reporting errors that HMRC flags
  • You value accuracy over familiarity
  • You’re approaching the VAT threshold and need precise turnover tracking

The Real Cost of Spreadsheets

The bridging software costs £48. The spreadsheet costs you time.

A property manager with 10 listings across two platforms might spend 8–12 hours per month maintaining spreadsheets — downloading reports, reconciling payouts, categorising expenses, merging platform data. At any reasonable hourly rate, that’s far more than the cost of automated software.

And that’s before you factor in the cost of errors. A missed transaction or incorrect categorisation can trigger HMRC penalties of up to 30% of additional tax due for careless errors.

What HMRC Requires

Regardless of which approach you choose, MTD for Income Tax requires:

  • Digital records of all income and expenses
  • Quarterly submissions summarising your property income
  • End-of-period statement (annual finalisation)
  • Compatible software — bridging software or full MTD-compatible platforms

Spreadsheets alone are not MTD-compliant. You need at least bridging software to submit digitally. That’s non-negotiable from April 2026.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I switch from bridging software to automated reporting later?

Yes. There’s no lock-in. If you start with bridging software and find the manual work is too much, you can switch to automated reporting at any time.

Do I need bridging software if I have an accountant?

Your accountant may handle MTD submissions for you. But they’ll still need compatible software — either bridging software linked to your spreadsheets, or an automated platform. Ask them what they use and whether it covers your needs.

Does bridging software handle multiple platforms?

Bridging software submits whatever’s in your spreadsheet. If you manually merge Airbnb and Booking.com data into one sheet, it’ll work. But you’re doing the merging yourself.

Is my data safe with automated reporting platforms?

Check each platform’s privacy policy and data handling. Most reputable providers use encrypted connections and don’t store your platform login credentials — they use OAuth or read-only API access.
