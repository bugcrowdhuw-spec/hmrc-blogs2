Last updated: March 2026

Spreadsheets are the default tool for tracking Airbnb income — but they’re not the best tool. Manual data entry, formula errors, and platform format mismatches make spreadsheets a bottleneck. Here’s how to track your Airbnb income without spreadsheets — and why you should.

Why Spreadsheets Stop Working

Spreadsheets work when you have one property on one platform. But add complexity and they break down:

Common Spreadsheet Errors

  • Missed transactions — forgetting to add a booking
  • Duplicate entries — same booking on two platforms counted twice
  • Gross vs net confusion — using payout instead of gross income
  • Currency errors — wrong conversion rates for international bookings
  • Broken formulas — one wrong cell throws off your totals

The Time Cost

Most property managers spend 2–4 hours per month maintaining spreadsheets. That’s 24–48 hours per year on data entry alone.

The Alternative: Purpose-Built Software

  2. Calculates gross income automatically — handles the net-to-gross conversion
  3. Matches properties across platforms — same property, different listings
  4. Tracks per property — separate records without manual splitting
  5. Generates HMRC-ready reports — SA105 figures or Digital Platform Reporting XML

What About Free Options?

Google Sheets / Excel

Free, but the time cost isn’t. If you value your time at £20/hour and spend 3 hours per month on spreadsheet tracking, that’s £720/year in lost time.

AirDNA / Mashvisor

These are analytics tools for market research — not income tracking for tax purposes. They don’t generate HMRC reports.

Bank Statements

Your bank shows net payouts, not gross income. You’ll miss the platform fees and won’t have the breakdown HMRC requires.

When You Still Might Use a Spreadsheet

Spreadsheets aren’t useless — they’re just not ideal as your primary tracking tool. You might use them for:

  • Quick calculations or estimates
  • Tracking non-platform income (direct bookings, cash payments)
  • Notes and planning
  • Backup records

But for the core job of tracking Airbnb income for HMRC, purpose-built software is faster and more accurate.
