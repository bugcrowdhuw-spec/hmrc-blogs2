Reporting Airbnb income for tax sounds simple — until you try it. Between platform fees, cleaning charges, currency conversions, and HMRC’s rules on what counts as taxable income, most Airbnb hosts end up confused. This guide covers the best software for Airbnb tax reporting in the UK, so you can file with confidence.

The Problem with Airbnb Tax Reporting

Airbnb gives you a payout. HMRC wants to know your gross income. These aren’t the same thing.

Your Airbnb payout already has fees deducted. But HMRC expects you to report gross income and claim fees as an expense separately. Get this wrong and your tax return won’t match what HMRC sees — and since 2024, they already have your data.

What Airbnb Reports to HMRC

Since January 2024, Airbnb reports your earnings to HMRC under OECD Digital Platform Reporting rules. This includes:

  • Your total gross earnings for the tax year
  • The number of transactions
  • Your identity and property details

HMRC cross-references this with your Self Assessment. If the numbers don’t match, expect questions.

What You Need to Track

To report Airbnb income correctly, you need records of:

  • Gross income — total before platform fees
  • Platform fees — Airbnb’s service charges
  • Allowable expenses — cleaning, maintenance, insurance, mortgage interest
  • Rental days — for each property
  • Personal use days — if you use the property yourself

For most hosts, this data is spread across Airbnb statements, bank accounts, and receipts. Pulling it together manually takes hours.

Step-by-Step: How to Report Airbnb Income

Step 1: Download Your Airbnb Data

Go to Airbnb → Transactions → Export CSV. Save this file.

Step 2: Gather Other Platform Data

If you list on Booking.com, VRBO, or others, download their reports too.

Step 3: Import into Your Software

Upload your CSVs to your chosen tool. If using HMRC Reporter, it handles the import and matching automatically.

Step 4: Review and Categorise

Check that income is categorised correctly. Add any allowable expenses (cleaning, maintenance, insurance).

Step 5: Generate Your Report

Your software should produce either:

  • A completed Self Assessment (SA105 property pages)
  • HMRC Digital Platform Reporting XML

Step 6: Submit to HMRC

File your Self Assessment by 31 January following the tax year.

Common Airbnb Tax Mistakes

  1. Reporting net income instead of gross — HMRC wants gross, fees are a separate expense
  2. Forgetting about other platforms — HMRC gets data from ALL platforms
  3. Missing the £1,000 allowance — If your gross income is under £1,000, you don’t need to report
  4. Not tracking personal use — Days you use the property yourself affect your claim
  5. Late filing — Penalties start at £100 and increase

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Does Airbnb send me a tax summary? A: Airbnb provides transaction history and payout summaries, but not a formal tax report. You need to calculate your own taxable income.

Q: Do I report Airbnb income if I also have a full-time job? A: Yes. Airbnb income is additional income. You must report it on your Self Assessment even if you pay PAYE on your salary.

Q: What’s the difference between gross and net Airbnb income? A: Gross income is the total booking amount before any deductions. Net income is what Airbnb pays you after fees. HMRC wants gross.

Q: Can I claim mortgage interest as an expense? A: Yes, but it’s treated as a tax credit (20%) rather than a direct deduction if you’re a basic rate taxpayer. Higher rate taxpayers have different rules.

Q: How does HMRC know about my Airbnb income? A: Since January 2024, Airbnb reports your earnings directly to HMRC under OECD Digital Platform Reporting rules.
