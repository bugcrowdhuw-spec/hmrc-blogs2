Do Airbnb Hosts Need to Report Income to HMRC?

Do Airbnb Hosts Need to Report Income to HMRC?

Short answer: Yes. But what does that mean in practice? Here’s what every UK Airbnb host must know.

The Simple Answer

Yes, You Must Report

Every UK Airbnb host must report income to HMRC:

  • Legal requirement — Tax law mandates reporting
  • Platform sharing — Airbnb reports your earnings
  • Cross-checking — HMRC compares data

Not reporting isn’t an option. The question is how much, not whether.

What HMRC Knows

Platforms share data automatically:

  • Total earnings — Yearly summary
  • Booking count — Nights booked
  • Property address — Your rental location
  • Payout details — Bank account information

This goes directly to HMRC. Your return will be checked.

Registration Requirements

When You Must Register

Register for Self Assessment if:

  • Income exceeds £1,000 — Net rental income
  • Above Personal Allowance — Combined with other income
  • Complex situation — Multiple properties, company

How to Register

  1. Visit GOV.UK — HMRC website
  2. Search “register for Self Assessment” — Find the form
  3. Provide details — Personal and income information
  4. Receive UTR — Your Unique Tax Reference
  5. Set up account — Government Gateway for filing

Registration Deadlines

Register:

  • By 5 October — For current tax year
  • As soon as income starts — Don’t wait

Income Thresholds

Key Thresholds

ThresholdAmountRequirement
Property Income Allowance£1,000Can claim, no tax if under
Personal Allowance£12,570No income tax below this
VAT Registration£85,000Must register for VAT

What These Mean

£1,000 Property Income Allowance:

  • If net rental income under £1,000
  • No tax to pay
  • Still must register and tell HMRC

Under £12,570 total income:

  • Income tax at 0%
  • But still file return

Over £85,000:

  • Must register for VAT -MTD becomes mandatory

Reporting What Income

What to Include

All rental-related income:

  • Platform bookings — Airbnb, Booking.com, Vrbo
  • Guest payments — Cleaning fees, extra guests
  • Direct bookings — Cash, bank transfer
  • Retained deposits — Kept from security

What NOT to Include

  • Deposits returned — Not income
  • Advance payments — For future stays
  • Gift cards received — Only when redeemed

How to Report

Self Assessment Process

  1. Gather records — Income and expense documentation
  2. Calculate profit — Income minus allowable expenses
  3. Log into HMRC — Government Gateway
  4. Complete SA Property — Property income section
  5. File return — By deadline (31 January)

What HMRC Expects

Your return must show:

  • Total income — All rental payments
  • Total expenses — Allowable deductions
  • Net profit — Taxable amount
  • Supporting records — Kept for audit

FAQ

What if I only earn a small amount?

Even small amounts should be reported. Claim the Property Income Allowance if under £1,000. Always tell HMRC.

Can I just not register and hope HMRC doesn’t notice?

No. Platform data is shared automatically. Not registering triggers investigation. Penalties and back taxes apply.

What happens if I forget to report one year?

File a correction as soon as possible. HMRC charges interest. Penalties may apply for late reporting. Act quickly.

Do I need an accountant?

Not necessarily. Simple returns can be filed directly. Accountants help with complex situations: multiple properties, company structures, tax planning.
