What Property Managers Need to Know About Making Tax Digital

Last updated: March 2026

Making Tax Digital is the biggest shift in UK tax reporting for property managers in decades. If you manage rental properties — whether long-term lets, short-term rentals, or a mix — you need to understand what’s changing and how it affects your day-to-day work. Here’s a clear breakdown of what you need to know.

The Headlines

  • MTD replaces Self Assessment for property income (phased rollout from April 2026)
  • Digital records are mandatory — paper and basic spreadsheets won’t cut it
  • Quarterly submissions replace one annual return
  • Software is required — you need MTD-compatible tools to submit

Who It Affects

Property Managers with Their Own Properties

If you own rental properties and your gross property income exceeds the threshold, MTD applies to you directly.

Property Managers Acting for Landlords

If you manage properties on behalf of other landlords, MTD affects your operations. You may need to:

  • Keep digital records per landlord
  • Submit quarterly updates on their behalf
  • Use software that supports multi-owner tracking

Estate Agents with Property Management

If your agency handles rent collection and property management, MTD changes your reporting obligations and the data you need to provide to landlords.

Note: Gross income means total rent received before any expenses. Many property managers are closer to these thresholds than they realise.

What Changes Day-to-Day

Record Keeping

Before: Monthly or annual spreadsheet updates, paper receipts.

After: Continuous digital recording. Every transaction — income and expense — must be recorded digitally with dates and categories.

Reporting

Before: One Self Assessment per year, due 31 January.

After: Quarterly submissions (every 3 months), plus an End of Period Statement and Final Declaration.

Software

Before: Optional. Many property managers used spreadsheets or paper.

After: Mandatory. You need MTD-compatible software that can submit directly to HMRC.

What You Need to Track

Per property, digitally:

  • Income: Rent received, platform income (Airbnb, Booking.com), service charges received
  • Expenses: Maintenance, repairs, insurance, mortgage interest, management fees, utilities
  • Property details: Address, ownership structure, letting type
  • Dates: When income was received, when expenses were incurred

The Quarterly Submission Cycle

How It Works

  1. Throughout the quarter: Record income and expenses digitally
  2. End of quarter: Review and finalise your records
  3. Within 1 month: Submit the quarterly update to HMRC
  4. After tax year: Submit End of Period Statement and Final Declaration

Common Concerns

“I manage 50 properties. This is going to be a nightmare.”

It doesn’t have to be. The right software handles the complexity. Tools like HMRC Reporter automate data import, property-level tracking, and report generation — so the workload stays manageable regardless of portfolio size.

“My landlords don’t understand MTD.”

You’ll need to educate them. Many landlords rely on their property manager to handle tax compliance. MTD means you need to provide quarterly data, not just an annual summary.

“I use multiple platforms (Airbnb, Booking.com, direct lets).”

MTD requires all income to be recorded digitally. If you receive income from multiple sources, you need software that consolidates across platforms.

“What about capital allowances and Furnished Holiday Let changes?”

The FHL regime was abolished from April 2025. FHLs are now treated as standard residential property income. MTD applies to both types equally.

How to Prepare

Immediate Actions

  1. Calculate your gross property income — check which threshold applies
  2. Audit your current systems — identify what needs to change
  3. Choose MTD software — research options now, don’t wait

Before Your MTD Start Date

  1. Digitise all records — move from paper/spreadsheet to software
  2. Set up properties — add details for each property
  3. Organise expenses — create consistent categories
  4. Practice quarterly reporting — run a test quarter

Ongoing

  1. Record transactions as they happen — don’t batch at quarter-end
  2. Review quarterly submissions — check for accuracy before submitting
  3. Keep receipts digitally — photos or scans of all expense receipts

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I need MTD software for each landlord I manage? A: You need software that can track each landlord’s properties separately. Multi-owner support is essential for property managers acting on behalf of others.

Q: What if a landlord’s income is below the threshold? A: MTD may not apply to them yet, but digital records are still good practice. When MTD expands to all property income (expected 2028), they’ll need to comply.

Q: Can I submit quarterly updates on behalf of my landlords? A: Yes, with their authorisation. You’ll need MTD-compatible software that supports agent submissions.

Q: What happens if I miss a quarterly deadline? A: You’ll earn penalty points. Reach the threshold (4 points for quarterly filers) and you’ll receive a £200 fine. Persistent failures trigger higher penalties.

Q: Does MTD apply to commercial property? A: MTD for Income Tax applies to property income. Commercial property income may fall under different rules depending on your structure. Check with your accountant.
