Airbnb Tax for UK Hosts: Complete Guide

Earning from Airbnb means paying tax. Here’s everything UK hosts need to know about their tax obligations.

Understanding Your Tax Position

When Airbnb Income Is Taxable

Tax applies when:

  • Income exceeds £1,000 — Per tax year
  • You’re above Personal Allowance — £12,570
  • You’re registered for Self Assessment — Required for complex returns

The £1,000 Property Income Allowance simplifies things:

  • Automatic — If income under £1,000
  • Optional — If income higher, you can still claim
  • Either/or — Compare: full reporting vs allowance

Tax Rates on Rental Income

Airbnb income adds to your other income:

Income BandTax Rate

Your total UK income determines your rate.

Taxable Income: What’s Included

All Money Received

Include all forms of payment:

  • Booking payments — Via Airbnb platform
  • Cleaning fees — Guest-paid
  • Late check-out fees — Additional charges
  • Security deposits — Retained deposits
  • Direct payments — Cash or bank transfer
  • Any other income — Related to hosting

Money Not Included

These aren’t taxable:

  • Refunded deposits — Returned to guests
  • Advance payments — For future stays
  • Airbnb gift cards — Not income

Capital vs Revenue

Distinguish correctly:

  • Revenue expenses — Deductible: repairs, cleaning
  • Capital expenses — Different treatment: extensions, major renovations

Mixed-use Expenses

If personal use occurred:

  • Apportion expenses — Split between personal and rental
  • Keep records — Document the split

Record-Keeping Essentials

Required Records

Keep for each booking:

  • Platform statement — Airbnb accounting
  • Bank statement — Payment confirmation
  • Receipts — All expenses
  • Calendar — Occupancy dates

Storage

Digital storage preferred (required for MTD):

  • Organise by date — Monthly folders
  • Separate by property — If multiple rentals
  • Backup regularly — Cloud storage
  • Keep 6 years — Minimum retention

Filing Your Return

Self Assessment Process

  1. Register for Self Assessment — If not already
  2. Gather records — All income and expenses
  3. Calculate profit — Income minus expenses
  4. Complete return — SA Property section
  5. File by deadline — 31 January online

FAQ

Can I claim the £1,000 Property Income Allowance?

Yes. If your net rental income is under £1,000, you can claim this allowance and pay no tax. But you must still register and tell HMRC.

What if I use my home for Airbnb sometimes?

This is the Rent a Room scheme. You can earn up to £7,500 tax-free if using the Simplified basis. Otherwise, calculate the proportion used for guests.

Can I offset losses against other income?

Property losses can usually offset against other property profits within the same property business. Limited relief against other income exists. Consult a tax adviser.

What happens if I don’t register?

Penalties apply for late registration. HMRC also receives platform data. Not registering looks suspicious and triggers investigation.
