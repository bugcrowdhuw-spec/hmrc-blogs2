# How to Report Airbnb Income to HMRC: UK Guide

**Airbnb income must be reported to HMRC — but how? Here's your complete guide to filing correctly.**

## Why Reporting Matters

### HMRC Knows About Your Airbnb Income

Platforms report data directly to HMRC:

- **Since 2023** — Digital platform reporting rules
- **OECD initiative** — Applies worldwide
- **Automatic sharing** — No audit required

Your Airbnb earnings are already known. What you report must match.

### What Happens If You Don't Report

Failure to report correctly triggers:

- **Automatic flags** — Platform vs return mismatch
- **Compliance checks** — HMRC requests records
- **Penalties** — Up to 30% of missing tax
- **Interest** — Daily accrual
- **Criminal investigation** — In serious cases

## Reporting Requirements

### Self Assessment Registration

First step: register for Self Assessment

1. **Visit gov.uk** — HMRC website
2. **Register for Self Assessment** — If not already
3. **Receive UTR** — Unique Tax Reference
4. **Set up Government Gateway** — For filing

### What to Report

On your Self Assessment return:

**Income Section (SA Property):**
- Total rental income received
- Less: allowable expenses
- = Net profit/loss

**Details Required:**
- Property address
- Rental period
- Income breakdown
- Expense breakdown

### Deadlines

| Deadline | What |
|----------|------|
| 31 Oct | Paper returns |
| 31 Jan | Online returns |
| Quarterly | If MTD |

## How to Calculate Your Income

### Income Sources to Include

All rental-related income:

- **Booking payments** — From Airbnb platform
- **Cleaning fees** — Guest payments
- **Late check-out fees** — Additional charges
- **Security deposits** — Retained (if withheld)
- **Direct payments** — Cash, bank transfer
- **Any other rental income** — Related to property

### Excluded Income

Don't include:

- **Deposits returned** — Not your income
- **Advance payments** — For future stays (deferred)
- **Airbnb gift cards** — Not taxable

### Example Calculation

| Income Type | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| Airbnb bookings | £15,000 |
| Cleaning fees | £800 |
| Late check-out | £200 |
| Direct payments | £500 |
| **Total Gross Income** | **£16,500** |

## Calculating Allowable Expenses

### Deductible Expenses

Reduce your tax with allowable expenses:

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Host fees** | Airbnb platform charges |
| **Cleaning** | Between-stay, deep cleans |
| **Linen** | Sheets, towels, toiletries |
| **Utilities** | Electric, gas, water, wifi |
| **Insurance** | Host protection |
| **Repairs** | Maintaining property |
| **Mortgage interest** | If applicable |
| **Agent fees** | Property management |

### Non-Deductible

These aren't allowable:

- **Personal expenses** — Not rental-related
- **Capital improvements** — Different treatment
- **Living expenses** — Personal costs
- **Depreciation** — Different rules apply

## Record-Keeping Requirements

### What to Keep

For each rental transaction:

- **Booking record** — Date, guest, amount
- **Payment confirmation** — Bank statement
- **Platform statement** — Airbnb summary
- **Receipts** — All expenses
- **Correspondence** — Guest communications

### How Long to Keep

Retain records for **6 years** from end of tax year:

- 2025-26 records: keep until 2032
- 2026-27 records: keep until 2033

### Digital Storage

MTD requires digital records:

- **Cloud storage** — Accessible, backed up
- **Organised** — By property, by date
- **Searchable** — Easy retrieval
- **Preserved** — Cannot be altered

## FAQ

### Do I need to register as self-employed for Airbnb income?

Yes. Register for Self Assessment if earning over £1,000 per year. Even below, consider registering to claim expenses.

### What if I earn under the Personal Allowance?

You still need to report. If under £1,000 with no expenses, the Property Income Allowance covers tax. But you must still tell HMRC.

### Can I offset losses against other income?

In specific circumstances, yes. Generally, property losses can offset against other property profits. Consult a tax adviser.

### How do I report if I have multiple properties?

Each property is a separate rental business. Report total income on Self Assessment, with property-level details.
