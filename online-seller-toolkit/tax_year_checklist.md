# Tax Year-End Checklist for Online Sellers

**Tax Year 2025/26**  
**Deadline: 5 April 2026**

---

## February-March Tasks

- [ ] Export all sales data from each platform
- [ ] Reconcile platform fees with your records
- [ ] Check all expense receipts
- [ ] Calculate total income by platform
- [ ] Determine if trading allowance or expenses better
- [ ] Check VAT threshold status
- [ ] Review CGT items (profit-making sales)
- [ ] Complete Self Assessment (if required)
- [ ] **Deadline: 31 January** (online filing)

## Trading Allowance Check

- [ ] Total income under £1,000 = no tax owed
- [ ] Over £1,000 = choose allowance OR expenses
- [ ] Remember: can't claim both

---

## VAT Check

- [ ] 12-month rolling total under £90,000 = no VAT needed
- [ ] Approaching threshold = plan for registration
- [ ] Already registered = submit VAT returns

---

## Records to Keep

- [ ] All platform statements
- [ ] Expense receipts (packaging, equipment, etc.)
- [ ] Proof of purchase for items sold
- [ ] Bank statements showing payouts
- [ ] Photos of items (for CGT proof)

---

## Common Mistakes

1. Forgetting platform fees reduce profit
2. Not tracking personal vs sourcing sales
3. Missing the 31 January deadline
4. Not realizing HMRC gets data from platforms
5. Confusing trading allowance with CGT allowance
