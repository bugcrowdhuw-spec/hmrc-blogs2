# MTD for Landlords and Property Managers Explained (2026 Guide)

<meta_description>MTD for landlords and property managers explained. Covers 2026 timeline, quarterly submissions, digital records, and MTD-compatible software.</meta_description>

*Last updated: March 2026*

Making Tax Digital for Income Tax is the biggest change to UK property taxation in years. Whether you're a landlord with a single buy-to-let or a property manager handling dozens of units, MTD will affect how you report to HMRC. This 2026 guide explains everything you need to know.

## What Is MTD for Income Tax?

Making Tax Digital for Income Tax replaces the annual Self Assessment with a digital-first system. Instead of one yearly return, you'll:

- Keep digital records of income and expenses
- Submit quarterly updates to HMRC
- File an End of Period Statement
- Submit a Final Declaration

The goal is more frequent, more accurate tax reporting using digital tools.

## Who Does MTD Apply To?

MTD for Income Tax applies to self-employed individuals and landlords based on their gross income (turnover, not profit).

### MTD Phase-In Timeline

| Gross income | MTD starts |
|---|---|
| Over £50,000 | April 2026 |
| Over £30,000 | April 2027 |
| Over £20,000 | Expected 2028 |

**Important:** The threshold is based on **gross income** (total rent before expenses), not profit. Many landlords are closer to the threshold than they think.

### Who's Exempt?

- Property income under £20,000 (for now)
- Those who qualify for a digital exclusion exemption (age, disability, remoteness)
- Trustees and non-resident companies (different rules apply)

## What Changes for Landlords Under MTD?

### Before MTD

- Paper or spreadsheet records accepted
- One annual Self Assessment (due 31 January)
- Summary-level figures accepted

### After MTD

- Digital records mandatory
- Quarterly submissions required
- Property-level detail required
- MTD-compatible software required

## How Does the MTD Process Work?

### 1. Digital Record Keeping

You must record digitally:

- Income per property (rent received, dates)
- Expenses per property (categorised)
- Property details (address, ownership percentage)
- Dates of all transactions

### 2. Quarterly Updates

Submit summaries every three months:

| Quarter | Period | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| Q1 | April – June | 7 August |
| Q2 | July – September | 7 November |
| Q3 | October – December | 7 February |
| Q4 | January – March | 7 May |

Each update is a summary of income and expenses for that quarter.

### 3. End of Period Statement

After the tax year ends, submit a final statement confirming your total income and expenses. Adjust for any differences between quarterly estimates and actual figures.

### 4. Final Declaration

A final submission combining all income sources — property, employment, savings — and calculating your total tax bill.

## What Does MTD Mean for Different Property Types?

### Long-Term Rentals (ASTs)

If you have one or two properties with stable monthly rent, MTD is manageable. Your quarterly submissions will be predictable. Record rent payments as they come in, track expenses per property, and submit quarterly summaries.

### Short-Term Rentals (Airbnb, Booking.com)

Short-term lets are more complex. High transaction volumes, multiple platforms, and variable income make manual tracking impractical. You need to import data from all platforms, calculate gross income before fees, and handle currency conversions. ## MTD Penalties: What Happens If You're Late?

MTD uses a points-based penalty system:

- **Late submission:** 1 point per late quarterly update
- **Points threshold:** 4 points for quarterly filers
- **Fine:** £200 once you hit the threshold
- **Persistent failures:** Additional penalties

Late payment penalties work differently:

- **2% penalty** if tax is 15+ days late
- **Additional 2%** if tax is 30+ days late
- **4% annualised** penalty if tax is 31+ days late

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the MTD threshold for landlords?

Gross property income over £50,000 from April 2026, over £30,000 from April 2027, and over £20,000 from expected 2028.

### Is Making Tax Digital mandatory for landlords?

Yes, for those above the income thresholds. HMRC expects to extend it to all property income by 2028.

### What records does MTD require?

Digital records of income, expenses, and property details — per property, with dates and categories.

### Can I still claim expenses under MTD?

Yes. MTD doesn't change what you can claim — it changes how you record and report it.
