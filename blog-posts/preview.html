How to Report Airbnb Income to HMRC: The Complete 2026 Guide

Last updated: March 2026

If you're renting out a property on Airbnb in the UK, you need to report your income to HMRC. Since January 2024, Airbnb (and other platforms) are legally required to share your earnings directly with HMRC — so there's no hiding from it. Here's exactly what you need to do.

Do I Need to Report My Airbnb Income?

Yes, if your total rental income exceeds £1,000 per year.

Your situationWhat to do
Income under £1,000/yearNo reporting needed (property income allowance)
Income £1,000–£2,500Contact HMRC, they may adjust your tax code
Income over £2,500You must file a Self Assessment tax return
Furnished Holiday Let (FHL)Report as business income with additional rules

What Changed in 2024?

Airbnb and other platforms now report your income to HMRC automatically. Under OECD Digital Platform Reporting rules, platforms must share your total earnings, number of bookings, and your identity/tax details.

How to Report: Step by Step

Step 1: Gather Your Records

  • Gross income from each platform
  • Allowable expenses (cleaning, maintenance, insurance, mortgage interest)
  • Property details (address, ownership split if joint)
  • Rental days for each property

Step 2: Calculate Your Taxable Income

Taxable income = Gross income - Allowable expenses

Step 3: File Your Self Assessment

Report on the SA105 (Property Income) supplementary pages.

Step 4: Pay Your Tax

Due by 31 January following the tax year.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Not declaring because Airbnb "handles it" — they report income, not file your return
  2. Forgetting about other platforms — HMRC gets data from ALL platforms
  3. Missing the property income allowance — under £1,000 = no reporting
  4. Claiming non-allowable expenses
  5. Late filing — penalties start at £100

FAQ

Q: How does HMRC know about my Airbnb income?
A: Since January 2024, platforms report your earnings under OECD Digital Platform Reporting rules.

Q: Will I be taxed on the full Airbnb payout or after fees?
A: Report gross income. Platform fees are an allowable expense you deduct.

HMRC Digital Platform Reporting: What UK Holiday Let Owners Need to Know

Last updated: March 2026

If you let property through Airbnb, Booking.com, VRBO, or any other online platform, there's a new reporting requirement you need to understand.

What Is Digital Platform Reporting?

Digital Platform Reporting (DPR) is part of the OECD's Model Reporting Rules for Digital Platforms. The UK adopted these rules in January 2024. The platform you use now tells HMRC exactly how much you've earned.

What Does This Mean for You?

  1. HMRC Already Knows Your Income — If your Self Assessment doesn't match, HMRC will notice.
  2. You Still Need to File Your Own Tax Return — The platform reports income, but YOU file and pay.
  3. Accuracy Matters More Than Ever — Discrepancies are easy to spot with standardised data.

Penalties for Non-Compliance

IssuePotential penalty
Careless errorUp to 30% of the tax due
Deliberate errorUp to 70% of the tax due
Deliberate + concealedUp to 100% of the tax due
Late filing£100 initial, then £10/day up to £900

Multiple Platforms

Each platform reports separately to HMRC. HMRC sees the combined total. You need to deduplicate and consolidate in your Self Assessment.
