# MTD for Landlords and Property Managers Explained (2026 Guide)

*Last updated: March 2026*

Making Tax Digital for Income Tax is the biggest change to UK property taxation in years. Whether you're a landlord with a single buy-to-let or a property manager handling dozens of units, MTD will affect how you report to HMRC. This 2026 guide explains everything you need to know.

## What Is MTD for Income Tax?

Making Tax Digital for Income Tax replaces the annual Self Assessment with a digital-first system. Instead of one yearly return, you'll:

- Keep digital records of income and expenses
- Submit quarterly updates to HMRC
- File an End of Period Statement
- Submit a Final Declaration

The goal: more frequent, more accurate tax reporting using digital tools.

## Who Does MTD Apply To?

MTD for Income Tax applies to self-employed individuals and landlords based on their gross income (turnover, not profit).

### MTD Phase-In Timeline

| Gross income | MTD starts |
|---|---|
| Over £50,000 | April 2026 |
| Over £30,000 | April 2027 |
| Over £20,000 | Expected 2028 |

**Important:** The threshold is based on **gross income** (total rent before expenses), not profit. Many landlords are closer to the threshold than they think.

### Who's Exempt?

- Property income under £20,000 (for now)
- Those who qualify for a digital exclusion exemption (age, disability, remoteness)
- Trustees and non-resident companies (different rules apply)

## What MTD Changes for Landlords

### Before MTD

- Paper or spreadsheet records accepted
- One annual Self Assessment (due 31 January)
- Summary-level figures accepted

### After MTD

- Digital records mandatory
- Quarterly submissions required
- Property-level detail required
- MTD-compatible software required

## The MTD Process: How It Works

### 1. Digital Record Keeping

You must record digitally:

- Income per property (rent received, dates)
- Expenses per property (categorised)
- Property details (address, ownership percentage)
- Dates of all transactions

### 2. Quarterly Updates

Submit summaries every three months:

| Quarter | Period | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| Q1 | April – June | 7 August |
| Q2 | July – September | 7 November |
| Q3 | October – December | 7 February |
| Q4 | January – March | 7 May |

Each update is a summary of income and expenses for that quarter.

### 3. End of Period Statement

After the tax year ends, submit a final statement confirming your total income and expenses. This is where you adjust for any differences between quarterly estimates and actual figures.

### 4. Final Declaration

A final submission combining all income sources — property, employment, savings, etc. — and calculating your total tax bill.

## What This Means for Different Property Types

### Long-Term Rentals (ASTs)

If you have one or two properties with stable monthly rent, MTD is manageable. Your quarterly submissions will be predictable.

**Key tasks:**

- Submit quarterly summaries

### Short-Term Rentals (Airbnb, Booking.com)

Short-term lets are more complex. High transaction volumes, multiple platforms, and variable income make manual tracking impractical.

- Submit quarterly summaries

## Common Questions About MTD

### "Do I need to submit every quarter if I have no income?"

Yes. You must submit a nil return for quarters with no income.

### "Can I use spreadsheets?"

Not directly. Spreadsheets don't meet MTD requirements unless connected to MTD bridging software. Purpose-built tools are recommended.

### "What if I make a mistake in a quarterly submission?"

You can correct errors in the next quarterly submission or in the End of Period Statement. MTD's penalty system is points-based — occasional mistakes are tolerated, but repeated failures trigger fines.

### "Does my accountant need to do this?"

Your accountant can submit on your behalf, but the records must be digital. Both you and your accountant need access to MTD-compatible software.

### "What about mortgage interest relief?"

Mortgage interest is claimed as a 20% tax credit for residential property. This doesn't change with MTD — you still track it as an expense.

## MTD Penalties

MTD uses a points-based penalty system:

- **Late submission:** 1 point per late quarterly update
- **Points threshold:** Varies by submission frequency (quarterly = 4 points)
- **Fine:** £200 once you hit the threshold
- **Persistent failures:** Additional penalties

Late payment penalties work differently:

- **2% penalty** if tax is 15+ days late
- **Additional 2%** if tax is 30+ days late
- **4% annualised** penalty if tax is 31+ days late

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is the MTD threshold for landlords?**
A: Gross property income over £50,000 from April 2026, over £30,000 from April 2027, and over £20,000 from expected 2028.

**Q: Is Making Tax Digital mandatory?**
A: Yes, for those above the income thresholds. HMRC expects to extend it to all property income by 2028.

**Q: What records does MTD require?**
A: Digital records of income, expenses, and property details — per property, with dates and categories.

**Q: Can I still claim expenses under MTD?**
A: Yes. MTD doesn't change what you can claim — it changes how you record and report it.
